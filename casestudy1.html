The Problem


My friend Sandra unintentionally presented me with a problem I could solve for her while our kids were playing one day. She is a photographer and wanted to really launch her business but she didn't quite know how to do it. She had many different social media outlets for her business, but she didn't update it consistently, plus she just wanted one central place to steer people to when they wanted a professional photographer. She also had many different colors and fonts and logos in many different places, such as on social media and business cards - nothing was consistent.


I presented the idea of having one central website, one central logo, one set of colors and fonts, and then the off-shoots of different social media platforms would unilaterally be updated and connected from there and vice-versa. She loved this idea, so I went to work!


Interview

At the discovery phase of my project, I conducted an interview with the business owner in order to get a better understanding of what she wanted out of her website.

  • What are the content pages you want for this website?
  • Home page (Landing page)
  • About Me page
  • Page to show photos of my studio
  • Contact page
  • Gallery - show an example of my photography skills
  • Schedule and/or pay for their session page
